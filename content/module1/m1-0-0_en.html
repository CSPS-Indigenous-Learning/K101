Chapter 1: Getting to know First Nations peoples

First Nations or Indians?

Let’s look at the terminology

You may have learned in school that early European explorers called the people they encountered “Indians” because they mistakenly believed that North America was India. This story may or may not be true, but the term “Indian” stuck. It was commonly used to refer to Indigenous populations until the 1970s.

Some Indigenous peoples in Canada chose the term “First Nation” as a way to refer to themselves. It is used today by government and the public as a more respectful term than “Indian.” Although it is widely used, no legal definition of “First Nation” exists.

In this course, we use “First Nation” rather than “Indian,” except in historical or legal references.

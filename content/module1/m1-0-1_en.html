What’s in a name?

There are many nations under the umbrella term “First Nations.” Each has its own distinct language, culture, kinship and traditional territory. When talking to First Nations people, try to use the specific name of their community or nation. If you don’t know it, it’s always better to ask than to assume.

One of the Elders we are privileged to work with at the Canada School of Public Service is Dominique (written T8aminik in Algonquin) Rankin or Kapiteotak (only available in French). He is:

  • Indigenous
  • First Nation
  • Algonquin
  • Anicinape
  • Dominique (written T8aminik in Algonquin), a name given to him by missionaries
  • Kapiteotak, a name given to him at birth by his parents

Self-reflection: If you were to meet this person, which name would you use and why?

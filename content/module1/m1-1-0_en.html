Where do First Nations peoples live in Canada?

First Nations peoples are present all across Canada.

In the 2016 Canadian Census, 1.67 million people identified as being Indigenous.

Increasing numbers

It can be difficult to come up with the actual number of First Nations in Canada. The history of the Indian Act and the band governance system have complicated this exercise significantly:

  • Many nations that historically existed were moved from their traditional territories
  • They were often broken into smaller groups and had different governance systems imposed

Rebuilding these historical nations is a key part of meaningful reconciliation.

According to the 1996 Report of the Royal Commission on Aboriginal Peoples, there were then “between 60 and 80 historically based nations in Canada…compared with a thousand or so local Aboriginal communities” (Vol. 2, 143). Today however, Indigenous Services Canada and Crown-Indigenous Relations and Northern Affairs Canada recognize 618 First Nations communities. The newest is the Oujé-Bougoumou Cree Nation in Quebec, which was legally recognized in October 2011.

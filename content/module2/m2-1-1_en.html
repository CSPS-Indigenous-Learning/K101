The reserve system

Under the 1876 Indian Act, the reserve system set aside a “piece of land, the legal title to which is vested in Her Majesty that has been set apart by Her Majesty for the use and benefit of a band.”

Historically, this has meant that First Nations had land set aside to live on, but ownership and control of the land remained with the Government of Canada. The reserve system served as an effective way to control First Nations peoples and their land.

First Nations reserves are located throughout Canada. For detailed information about them and First Nations communities today, see the First Nation Profiles Interactive Map.

Did you know?

Not all First Nations communities are called reserves, such as those in the Northwest Territories and under self-government.

Imposed governance

When the Indian Act was introduced, the existing governance structures were forcibly replaced with an imposed governance called the Indian Band Election Regulations. In some cases, the Government of Canada with the military and traders appointed chiefs that reinforced the leadership of the men they liked to work with.

Today, the majority of First Nations governments are composed of a chief and councillors, who are responsible for making decisions on behalf of the First Nations community.

The selection of the chief and councillors is held in one of four ways:

  • following the steps outlined in the Indian Act and the Indian Band Election Regulations
  • using the new First Nations Elections Act, which is optional
  • according to a community's constitution as part of a self-government agreement
  • using a community leadership selection process (also called “band custom”)

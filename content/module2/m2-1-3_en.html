Indian registration (status)

“Registered Indians” are First Nations individuals who are registered with the Government of Canada under the Indian Act. They are often referred to as “status Indians”, although that is not an official term.

Section 6 of the current Indian Act sets out the rules for eligibility. Eligibility for Indian status is based on an individual's descent from a person who is registered, or eligible to be registered, as an Indian.

The definition of who is an “Indian” has historically been determined by the Government of Canada and not by the First Nations. Historically, First Nations individuals could lose their status:

  • if they graduated from university
  • became Christian ministers
  • achieved professional designations as doctors or lawyers
  • served in the military

First Nations women and their children would lose their status if the women married men who were not First Nations.

Myth: All First Nations don’t pay taxes

A limited number of First Nations people do not pay taxes. Sections 87 to 90 of the Indian Act state that Status Indians do not pay federal or provincial taxes on real and personal property that is on a reserve.

Status Indians who work on a reserve do not pay federal or provincial taxes on income. However, any income earned off reserves is taxable.

Section 87 exempts GST on the goods and services bought by Status Indians at businesses located on a reserve. Goods and services purchased off reserves by Status Indians, but delivered to the reserve, are also tax exempt. Most provincial sales taxes are applied.

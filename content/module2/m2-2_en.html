What kinds of restrictions did the Indian Act put onto First Nations?

Answer: All of these

Restrictions included:

  • denying women Indian status (1876–1985)
  • implementing the Indian Residential Schools system (1876–1996)
  • restricting First Nations from leaving the reserve without an Indian agent’s permission (1884–1951)
  • stopping First Nations from forming political organizations (1923–1951)
  • forbidding First Nations’ from practicing their traditional beliefs and practices (1884–1951)
  • denying First Nations the right to vote (1880–1960)
  • preventing First Nations from selling agricultural produce (1884–2015)

Question of

True or False?

We could define a ‘’reserve’’ as land set aside for First Nations to live on but owned and controlled by the Government of Canada.

Answer: True

Historically, this has meant that First Nations had land set aside to live on, but ownership and control of the land remained with the Government of Canada. The reserve system served as an effective way to control First Nations peoples and their land.

Question of

True or False?

The Government of Canada decides according to the Indian Act who is a registered First Nations person and who is not.

Answer: True

Section 6 of the current Indian Act sets out the rules for eligibility. Eligibility for Indian status is based on an individual's descent from a person who is registered, or eligible to be registered, as an Indian.

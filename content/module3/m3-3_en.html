Treaties and comprehensive land claims

Before the Europeans arrived, First Nations had agreements among themselves over access to land and resources and the conduct of trade. Treaty making was a serious and solemn process. The completion of treaties involved ceremonies and took on a sacred meaning within society. When Europeans arrived on Turtle Island, First Nations continued a practice they were familiar with.

Wampum belts

Wampum belts were used in treaty making by many First Nations in the northeastern part of the country to signify peaceful intent or the importance of a message. For example, the Two Row Wampum Belt (Kaswehntha) was created by the Haudenosaunee and used over 400 years ago in dealings with the first newcomers.

The Two Row Wampum Belt embodies peace, friendship and the river of life.

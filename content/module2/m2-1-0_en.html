The Indian Act

Over the years, the Act has imposed many restrictions, which have negatively impacted First Nations for more than 150 years. Restrictions included:

    1876
  • denying women Indian status (1876–1985)
  • implementing the Indian Residential Schools system (1876–1996)
    • 1880
  • denying First Nations the right to vote (1880–1960)
    • 1884
  • restricting First Nations from leaving the reserve without an Indian agent’s permission (1884–1951)
  • forbidding First Nations’ from practicing their traditional beliefs and practices (1884–1951)
  • preventing First Nations from selling agricultural produce (1884–2015)
    • 1923
  • stopping First Nations from forming political organizations (1923–1951)

Self-reflection: Were you aware of the restrictions that the Indian Act placed on First Nations? How do you think the Act impacts the social realities of First Nations people today?

Can you imagine if the Government of Canada announced that you and your family could no longer:

  • vote
  • live in the city
  • celebrate your traditions
  • go to any of the food markets in the area
  • go to church, temple, mosque or shrine

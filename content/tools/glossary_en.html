Glossary

  • Intergenerational trauma

    A collective complex trauma inflicted on a group of people who share a specific group identity or affiliation—ethnicity, nationality or religious affiliation. It is the legacy of numerous traumatic events a community experiences over generations and encompasses the psychological and social responses to such events (Evans-Campbell, Journal of Interpersonal Violence 2008, p.320).

    Source: University of Calgary, Aboriginal Intergenerational Trauma

  • Red River Resistance

    A colony of farmers and hunters, many of them Métis, occupied a corner of Rupert's Land and feared for their culture and land rights. Métis resisted and declared a provisional government to negotiate.
